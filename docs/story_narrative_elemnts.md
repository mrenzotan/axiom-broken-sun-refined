# **Tutorial Stage — “The Shattered Apothecary”**

### **Opening Cutscene**

Black screen.  
 A low hum. Cracking glass. A distant voice echoing chemical names like prayers.

Light fades in.

Kaelen lies on the floor of a ruined apothecary. The ceiling is torn open to a fractured sky. Bottles float slowly in the air, droplets hanging motionless. His left arm glows crystalline.

He gasps awake.

A flicker of instinct — he **sees** the world differently now. The spilled liquids are not colors… but **acids**, **bases**, **water**, **fuel oils**, **dissolved salts**.

A whisper slips from his mouth.

“React…”

Nothing happens.

Then louder, uncertain:

“React?”

A nearby puddle bubbles briefly.

## **End of Tutorial Cutscene**

Kaelen steps onto a floating island outside the ruined shop.

The sun is broken. Islands drift in the void. Distant creatures howl.

Kaelen looks at his crystalline arm.

Quietly:

“So… the world still obeys rules.

*Transition to Level 1*

## **Level 1**

*Kaelen walks forward and sees a girl who stops him from going further.*

Senna : "Wait. It's dangerous to go any further. Ever since the sun shattered to pieces strange creatures have been showing up."

Kaelen: "Strange creatures? What do they look like?"

Senna : "They look like amalgamations of beasts and incomplete reactions. Reactions forced into perpetual motion by a force that refuses to let them settle."

*Upon hearing this, one word popped into kaelen's head;*

Kaelen: "Glimmerlings..."

Senna : "You know what they are?"

Kaelen: "Not exactly, that word just popped into my head for some reason."

Senna : "Either way, with those things walking around, you'd be better off just turning back"

Kaelen: "Well lucky for me I don't have anywhere to go back to. Don't worry I can take care of myself."

## **Level 1 - Upon reaching the first puzzle (Ice wall)**

*Kaelen stops before a towering wall of crystalline ice. Wind whistles across its jagged surface. Beyond it, a narrow path continues forward.*

Kaelen: "Frozen solid... but only frozen."

*Kaelen reaches his catalyst arm toward the ice wall*

Kaelen: "Based on everything I learned in the apothecary... substances change. They transform. Ice is no exception."

Kaelen: "The question isn't whether this wall cna be moved. The question is... what does it need?"

Kaelen: "Heat moves through materials. It breaks bonds. It changes what things are. A substance locked in one state won't stay locked if the conditions shift."

Kaelen: "Maybe I should think about what reacts to cold... and what chases it away."

Kaelen: "The Catalyst Arm knows more spells than the ones I've spoken aloud. I just have to remember what I felt the world teach me."

## **Level 1 - Second puzzle (Floating pools of water)**

*Kaelen approaches a chasm with several floating pools of water suspended in mid-air. Below is only void. The water ripples slightly, unstable and weightless.*

Kaelen: "Water... just drifting. No current. No pressure pulling it down."

Kaelen: "Before the Cascade, water had weight. It fell. It settled. Now it just... floats."

Kaelen: "But water is still water. It still follows the rules of what it is. Temperature, pressure, state... these still matter."

Kaelen: "If I had solid ground beneath me I could cross. But this is liquid. Unstable. Formless."

Kaelen: "Liquid and solid are not different substances. Thery're the same substance... just changed.

Kaelen: "What hardens water? What transforms it into something that can hold weight... somethins that can be crossed?

Kaelen: "The opposite of heat... the antithesis of fire. I felt it once, before the Cascade. A force that stops motion. That locks things in place."

## **Level 1 - Boss Encounter: Frost-Melt Sentinel**

*Kaelen enters a vast frozen chamber. Towering ice pillars surround him. The ground beneath is slick with a thin layer of water. The air is bitterly cold.*

*A massive shape emerges from the mist — a beast of pure crystalline ice. Its body is bulbous and jagged, covered in sharp protrusions and spikes that gleam like shards of glass. Patches of liquid water flow across its surface before freezing solid again. The creature is constantly transforming — ice melting into rivulets, then refreezing into new crystalline formations. Its size is immense, filling half the chamber.*

Kaelen: "What... are you?"

The creature roars — a sound like avalanches and glaciers calving at once.

Kaelen: "You're not like the Glimmerlings. You're... organized. Intentional."

*The beast shifts again — ice cracks and reforms, liquid pools freeze and shatter. Jagged spikes break and regrow from its body in an endless, violent cycle.*

Kaelen: "A Theorem. That's what the girl called it. A reaction forced into perpetual motion."

*The creature cycles between states, ice and liquid battling for dominance across its form.*

Kaelen: "But you're different. You're not chaotic. You're trapped in a pattern. Cycling between two states... never settling into one."

*The creature lunges forward, trailing both ice and water, its spikes glinting dangerously.*

Kaelen: "You exist between states. Neither solid nor liquid. Both. Neither."

Kaelen: "I don't know if I can destroy what you are. But I know one thing — every substance has rules. Every transformation has conditions."

Kaelen: "Maybe... if I can force you to a state... if I can interrupt your cycle..."

*The creature shifts again, waiting, its jagged form casting sharp shadows.*

Kaelen: "Then we'll both understand what you really are."

## **Level 1 - After Defeating the Frost-Melt Sentinel (SOLO SCENARIO)**

*The beast collapses. Its body convulses — ice shatters, water boils away into vapor, only to refreeze and shatter again. The cycle is breaking down. The creature writhes, trapped between states, unable to settle.*

*Kaelen stands breathing heavily, crystalline arm still glowing with residual reaction energy. He watches as the beast weakens, its form becoming less stable with each moment.*

Kaelen: "It's over. Your cycle... is finally breaking."

*The creature's roar becomes a sound of anguish — not hunger, but suffering. The ice and liquid within it continue their violent war, tearing it apart.*

Kaelen hesitates for a moment. His hand glows softly with potential reactions.

Kaelen: "I could end this. Give it peace. Or..."

He watches the creature thrash, unable to choose between its two natures.

Kaelen: "Or let it choose."

He lowers his arm, stepping back. He does not cast the Stabilize reaction.

*The creature's convulsions continue. Its form fractures further. But slowly, as if through sheer will, something changes. The ice and liquid begin to separate. Not blend, not war — but divide.*

The creature's form begins to split.

Kaelen: "You can't be both. So you'll be neither... or maybe something new entirely."

*The creature releases one final roar — not of rage, but of release. The ice falls away like scales. The liquid evaporates into mist. What remains is smaller, simpler, something barely recognizable as the beast that was.*

*A crystalline core sits in the center of the dissipating mist. It pulses faintly. Once, twice. Then goes still.*

*Kaelen approaches cautiously. The core is cold but not hostile. It pulses again.*

Kaelen: "You found your answer. Neither ice nor water. Just... essence."

He carefully retrieves the core. It's smooth, fractured in some places, but solid. Real.

Kaelen: "Thank you. For showing me what I'm capable of. And what happens when understanding isn't enough."

He places the core in his pack. It continues to pulse softly, like a second heartbeat.

Kaelen: "Maybe not all Theorems can be balanced. Maybe some need to learn what they truly are... alone."

He looks out at the frozen chamber, now eerily quiet.

Kaelen: "The Null-King destroys. I thought I could transform everything. But maybe... the world needs both choice and consequence."

## **Level 2**

*Kaelen emerges onto a plateau where charred earth and smoldering rocks are scattered. In the distance, heat distorts the air. A figure tends to a small, carefully controlled fire in a stone pit*

Kaelen: "Another person... alive out here?"

Ember: "Step back. Fire out here doesn't follow the old rules anymore. Since the Cascade, flames don't just warm — they rage. They spread where they shouldn't. They refuse to die when you need them to."

Kaelen: "You understand combustion?"

Ember: "I survived it. Before the Cascade, I kept furnaces. Fuel, oxygen, heat — I knew how to balance them. But now... the reactions are wild."

Ember: "There's a Theorem ahead. A creature made entirely of uncontrolled combustion. It destroyed the rest of my settlement. It's called the Living Furnace. If you're wise, you'll turn back."

Kaelen: "I can control reactions. With my voice."

Ember: "Can you? Or will you just unleash more chaos? Either way... maybe understanding is what we need now. The path ahead is dangerous. You'll know when you're close."

## **Level 2 - First Puzzle (Destroying the Bristle Wall)**

*Kaelen approaches a towering wall made of thorny, wooden bristles. They're too dense to push through, too thick to burn directly. But ahead of the wall, wisps of combustible gas drift from cracks in the ground.*

Kaelen: "A wall of thorns... locked in place. Solid. Impenetrable."

*He studies the gas vents.*

Kaelen: "But there's fuel here. Invisible. Waiting."

*He approaches the gas carefully.*

Kaelen: "Combustion needs three things — fuel, oxygen, and heat. The gas is here. The air surrounds it. All it needs is ignition."

*He raises his crystalline arm.*

Kaelen: "If I spark the gas... the explosion will release energy. Force. Enough to shatter wood?"

*He focuses.*

Kaelen: "Fire isn't just destruction. It's expansion. Pressure. A reaction that *pushes*."

## **Level 2 - Second Puzzle (Gas Propulsion)**

*Kaelen enters a vertical chamber. A platform is high above, unreachable by jumping. Below, more combustible gas vents from the floor.*

Kaelen: "Rising. Gas lifts when heated. Fire creates pressure that pushes upward."

*He watches the gas drift upward naturally.*

Kaelen: "Before the Cascade, I learned about buoyancy. About force. Hot air rises because it's less dense than cold air."

*He positions himself carefully above the gas vents.*

Kaelen: "If I ignite the gas beneath me... the explosion will push me upward. Not destroy me. Direct me."

*He breathes deeply.*

Kaelen: "Combustion as propulsion. Fire as a tool, not a weapon."

## **Level 2 - Final Puzzle (The Four Nodes)**

*Kaelen reaches a sealed door. Before it stand four crystalline nodes, dark and dormant. They're arranged in a pattern. Above the door, a inscription glows faintly.*

Kaelen: "Four points. Four places to ignite."

*He approaches the first node carefully.*

Kaelen: "These are designed to burn. They're waiting for fire."

*As he ignites the first node, it blazes with an intense flame. But then, movement. A creature emerges — something made of mist and shadow, barely visible. An **Extinguisher**, drawn to the flames.*

Kaelen: "What... are you?"

*The creature moves toward the burning node, not toward Kaelen. It focuses entirely on the flame, attempting to smother it.*

Kaelen: "You're drawn to fire. But you don't attack me... you attack the *reaction*."

*He moves quickly to ignite the second node.*

Kaelen: "I see. You're bound to the flames. Compelled to extinguish them. But you can't be everywhere at once."

*The Extinguisher hesitates — torn between the first and second burning nodes.*

Kaelen: "This is a puzzle within a puzzle. I have to stay ahead of it. I have to keep lighting fires faster than it can extinguish them."

*He races to the third node.*

Kaelen: "Combustion is about timing. About understanding *when* to react. Not just *how*."

*The Extinguisher moves desperately, but it's too slow.*

Kaelen: "The fire I create is faster than the force trying to erase it."

*He ignites the final node. All four burn simultaneously. The Extinguisher releases a sound of anguish — overwhelmed, unable to stop them all. The door glows and begins to open.*

Kaelen: *(breathing heavily)* "You tried to maintain a balance. To keep the reactions contained. But some reactions... are meant to spread."

## **Level 2 - Boss Encounter: The Living Furnace**

*Kaelen enters a massive chamber. The walls glow red. Cracks of flame seep from the stone itself. In the center, a creature of pure fire — bulbous and radiating heat, with veins of molten fuel running through its form. It is not solid, not liquid, but **pure energy given form**. When it moves, trails of flame follow. The very air shimmers.*

Kaelen: *(shielding his face from the heat)* "What *are* you?"

*The Furnace roars — a sound like a wildfire consuming a forest.*

Kaelen: "Not a Theorem trapped between states. You're... all of them at once. Fire is gas and heat and light and destruction all together."

*The creature pulses with intensity.*

Kaelen: "You're uncontrolled combustion given consciousness. Every reaction you are is *explosive*. Every moment you exist is consumption."

*Flames erupt around the chamber.*

Kaelen: "The Sentinel taught me about balance. About coexistence. But you... you teach something else."

*He steps back, analyzing.*

Kaelen: "You show me what happens when reaction becomes the *only* state. When there's no moderation. No limit. No purpose except to burn and spread."

*He raises his crystalline arm.*

Kaelen: "Maybe you can't be balanced like the Sentinel. Maybe you need to be *directed*. Taught that reaction has purpose beyond itself."

*The creature surges forward.*

Kaelen: "Let's see if I can teach you that lesson."

## **Level 2 - After Defeating the Living Furnace (SOLO SCENARIO)**

*The Furnace collapses, its form destabilizing. Flames erupt chaotically, uncontrolled. The creature writhes as its own combustion consumes it from within.*

Kaelen watches, breathing heavily, as the beast's own fire tears it apart.

Kaelen: "You were nothing but reaction. Pure, mindless combustion."

*The flames spread wildly, consuming the creature faster.*

Kaelen: "I could stabilize you. Give you purpose. But should I?"

*He raises his arm, ready to cast Stabilize. But something stops him.*

Kaelen: "The Sentinel learned from me. But you... you don't want to learn. You *are* reaction. You don't understand control because you've never known anything but burning."

*He lowers his arm.*

Kaelen: "Maybe some things can't be taught. Maybe some reactions need to burn themselves out."

*The creature releases one final, anguished roar as its own fire consumes it completely. Its form dissolves into ash and ember, scattering on the wind.*

*But in the center of the inferno, something survives — a crystalline fragment of pure fuel, glowing like a coal that refuses to die.*

Kaelen approaches carefully. The fragment is hot but manageable. He retrieves it.

Kaelen: "Your essence. The pure reaction, distilled."

*He holds it carefully. It pulses with heat.*

Kaelen: "Fire doesn't need to destroy everything. But it also can't be tamed by kindness alone. Sometimes... the only teaching is letting it burn."

## **Level 3**

*Kaelen enters a landscape scarred by acid damage. The ground is pitted and eaten away. Vegetation is dead. The air smells sharp and chemical. A figure is trapped on a small island of solid ground, surrounded by spreading acid pools.*

Kaelen: "Someone's stranded..."

*The figure looks up desperately. Their name is Tavin, a worker from a settlement that was destroyed by acid.*

Tavin: "Please... don't get closer. The ground — it's being eaten away. Everything the acid touches dissolves."

Kaelen: "What happened here?"

Tavin: "After the Cascade, acid started pooling everywhere. At first it was okay. But then... a Theorem emerged. The Corrosion Queen. It spreads acid. It controls it. Everything it touches becomes poisoned."

Kaelen: "Can it be stopped?"

Tavin: "If you can... *neutralize* it. Before the Cascade, I worked in refineries. Acid and base... they cancel each other out. If you understand that, maybe you can reach it. But first, you have to cross this wasteland."

Kaelen: "I understand chemistry. Acids have an opposite."

Tavin: "Bases. Find the right balance. And if you succeed... save us. Please."

## **Level 3 - First Puzzle (Acid Pool Neutralization)**

*Kaelen approaches a large pool of acidic water that blocks his path. The ground around it is eaten away and crumbling. But nearby, he finds deposits of alkaline powder (a base).*

Kaelen: "Acid. It eats things. It dissolves them."

Kaelen: "But acid has an opposite. Acid and base... they cancel each other out. They become something new. Something safe."

Kaelen: "Too much acid eats everything. Too much base and the balance flips the other way. But when they're equal... the water becomes safe."

Kaelen: "Not all at once. Slowly. Watching. Measuring."

*As he adds it, the acid bubbles and calms down. The poisoned water becomes normal water.*

Kaelen: "There. Balanced. The acid is no longer dangerous."

## **Level 3 - Second Puzzle (pH Balance Platform)**

*Kaelen encounters a crumbling stone platform suspended over a river of acid. The platform has two nodes on it, each one glowing with different acid levels. Below, the acid river bubbles and churns.*

Kaelen: "A bridge over poison. The platform is breaking."

*He examines the nodes. Some are bright red (too acidic), some are dim blue (too basic).*

Kaelen: "These nodes... they're measuring pH levels. The platform is falling apart because the acid and base aren't equal."

*He looks at the timing. The platform shakes.*

Kaelen: "The stone can only hold so long. I need to use spells on the right nodes. The acidic ones need base. The basic ones need acid."

Kaelen: "But I have to be fast. If the platform falls apart before I balance them all... I fall into the acid river."

Kaelen: "This is precision. Not like with fire or ice. Here I need to understand *exactly* what each node needs. Too much of one reaction and the balance flips the other way."

## **Level 3 - Boss Encounter: The Corrosion Queen**

*Kaelen enters a large chamber where the ground is covered in acid pools. The air smells sharp and burns. At the center, a creature emerges — the Corrosion Queen. Its body is liquid acid held inside a crystal shell. Vapor rises from it. Where it moves, the ground dissolves.*

Kaelen: "You're not like the others. The Sentinel was stuck between two forms. The Furnace was pure fire. But you... you are *eating away* at everything.

*The creature moves, and more ground dissolves.*

Kaelen: "You spread poison. You break down everything into nothing."

Kaelen: "But even acids follow rules. They have strength. And they have an opposite."

Kaelen: "I understand the Sentinel through balance. I understood the Furnace through how it burns. And you... I understand through what happens when there's too much of one thing."

Kaelen: "To stop excess, you need the opposite. To stop eating away, you need building up."

## **Level 3 - After Defeating the Corrosion Queen (SOLO SCENARIO)**

*The Corrosion Queen collapses. Its acid body starts to eat itself, bubbling and breaking down. The creature writhes as its own acid consumes it.*

Kaelen: "You were eating away without end. Breaking down everything."

*The creature falls apart further.*

Kaelen: "The Sentinel taught me balance. The Furnace taught me how to direct fire. But you... you represent something else."

*Kaelent watches as the creature dissolves.*

Kaelen: "Not all excess can be controlled. Some breaking down can't be stopped."

Kaelen: "Maybe I was wrong to think I could save everything. Maybe some things are meant to end."

*The creature releases one final, sad sound as the acid consumes the last of its crystal form. What remains is only acidic liquid, running across the ground.*

*But as it spreads, something remains — a crystal fragment, harder and purer than before, as if the creature's true nature was distilled by the very acid that destroyed it.*

Kaelen: "Your essence. Acid without excess. Pure... but powerless."

Kaelen: "Understanding doesn't mean saving everything. Sometimes it means accepting when something has to end."

## **Level 4 - Final Battle**

### **SOLO SCENARIO - The Core Chamber**

*Kaelen enters a long, narrow corridor. The walls are made of cracked stone. At the end, a massive door stands sealed. Before it, a chamber with three pedestals arranged in a triangle. Each pedestal glows with a different light.*

Kaelen: "This is it. The path to the Null-King."

*He retrieves the three cores he collected from his previous battles.*

Kaelen: "Phase change. Combustion. Acid and base. Three laws of chemistry. Three Theorems I learned from."

*He approaches the first pedestal — it glows with icy blue light. He places the core from the Frost-Melt Sentinel.*

Kaelen: "The Sentinel taught me that balance isn't weakness. It's control. It's understanding that two opposite things can coexist."

*He approaches the second pedestal — it glows with orange-red light. He places the fragment from the Living Furnace.*

Kaelen: "The Furnace taught me that pure reaction without direction is chaos. But that same reaction, controlled... becomes power."

*He approaches the third pedestal — it glows with sickly green light. He places the fragment from the Corrosion Queen.*

Kaelen: "The Queen taught me that even dissolution can be understood. That excess needs opposition. That some things can't be saved."

*The massive door swings open.*

*Beyond it, an endless chamber stretches. The sky inside is broken and fractured. At the center, a figure made of absolute stillness waits.*

Kaelen: "I came here understanding reactions. Understanding that the world has rules. That chemistry teaches us how matter moves."

Kaelen: "But now I understand something more. The Null-King doesn't want to control reactions. It wants to end them. To stop the world from changing. To make everything still."

Kaelen: "The Theorems I defeated taught me that reactions *can* be balanced. That chaos can become purpose. I'm not coming here to destroy. I'm coming here to teach you what I've learned."

Kaelen: "Let's show you what happens when someone understands not how to end reactions... but how to guide them."

---

## **Level 4 - After Defeating the Null-King (SOLO SCENARIO)**

*The Null-King collapses. Its form of pure stillness begins to unravel. The absolute absence of motion becomes motion itself. The creature crumbles, dissolves, becomes nothing.*

*Kaelen stands in the center of the endless chamber. The broken sky above him begins to shift. Cracks form... but not in the jagged way they were before. The light changes. Becomes warmer.*

Kaelen: "It's over. The Null-King... it's gone."

*He looks at his crystalline arm. It glows with a soft, steady light.*

Kaelen: "I didn't defeat it by destroying it. I defeated it by understanding what it represented. The refusal to change. The fear of reaction."

Kaelen: "The Null-King taught me that the world *needs* to change. That stillness isn't peace. It's death."

*The chamber begins to stabilize. The broken, fractured sky forms new light.*

Kaelen: "I started as an apprentice that only understood chemistry. But I'm something more now. I understand that reactions aren't just about substances. They're about *life*. About how things become what they're meant to be."

Kaelen: "The world is broken. But it can be healed. Not by stopping reactions. By guiding them. By understanding that every change follows rules. Every transformation has reason."

Kaelen: "The Cascade shattered the world. But it also gave me the power to understand how to fix it. Maybe that was the point all along."

*He raises his crystalline arm toward the new light.*

Kaelen: "Not as a weapon. But as a tool. A guide. The voice that names reactions and gives them purpose."

Kaelen: "There's still so much to learn. So many people who need help understanding the new rules. But now I know that understanding is stronger than destruction."

Kaelen: "The world obeyed rules before the Cascade. It obeys rules now. I just had to learn to read them. And to teach others that the world doesn't need to be afraid of change."

Kaelen: "We just need to learn how to guide it."
